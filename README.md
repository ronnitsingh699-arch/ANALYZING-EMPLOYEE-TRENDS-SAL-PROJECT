### Analyzing Employee Trends Using SQL – Project Description 

* Developed an end-to-end **Employee Trends Analysis** project using **SQL (MySQL)** to analyze workforce behavior and HR metrics. 
* Worked with an HR dataset containing **1,470 employee records** and 15 attributes. 
* Analyzed employee demographics including **age, gender, marital status, education level, and age bands**. 
* Evaluated employee distribution across departments such as **Sales, R&D, and Human Resources**. 
* Identified the most common job roles within each department using SQL aggregation queries. 
* Calculated average employee age by department and job satisfaction level to uncover workforce trends.  
* Analyzed job satisfaction across different education levels to understand employee engagement patterns. 
* Measured attrition rates by age group using SQL **CASE statements** and aggregate functions. 
* Identified departments with the highest and lowest employee satisfaction levels. 
* Investigated age bands and education categories experiencing the highest attrition rates. 
* Examined the impact of business travel frequency on employee satisfaction and engagement. 
* Analyzed satisfaction levels among married employees to identify workforce behavior patterns. 
* Utilized SQL functions including **COUNT(), AVG(), GROUP BY, ORDER BY, CASE, and Aggregate Functions** for data analysis. 
* Generated actionable insights to support **employee retention, workforce planning, and HR decision-making**.  
* Transformed raw HR data into meaningful business insights, demonstrating practical SQL and data analytics skills.
